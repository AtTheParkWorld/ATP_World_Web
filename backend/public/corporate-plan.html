The 90 days
before launch.

A realistic execution plan to take ATP Corporative Wellness from concept (today) to first paying pilot (September 2026). Designed to address the 10 failure modes identified in the pre-mortem. Lean, sequential, with explicit kill criteria at each phase so the program can be paused or pivoted before it eats the founder's time.

No B2B sales hire yet No expensive collateral Founder-led sales 3 pilots by Oct 31 1 paid customer by Dec 1
0
Foundations
Weeks 1-3 · mid-June to early-July

Stop selling something we can't deliver. Build the minimum viable product + paperwork. Talk to 15 HR leads to validate pricing, language, objections.

Week 1 — Customer discovery
  • Interview 15 HR / People leads in UAE (target: companies 50-500 employees)
  • Ask: current wellness spend, what's not working, what would make them switch
  • Validate "Pay as you move" vs fixed-fee preference; document objections verbatim
  • Identify the 3-5 ICP segments (e.g. mid-size tech, professional services, hospitality HQs)
Week 2 — Product & dashboard build
  • Build the HR dashboard MVP: company-scoped login, active employee count, sessions attended, leaderboard view, monthly PDF export
  • Build company onboarding flow: /corporate sign-up form, CSV upload + email domain auto-detect
  • Build company-scoped data isolation (no employee can see another company's data)
  • Ship the email magic-link enrollment flow for employees (re-use existing magic link infra)
Week 3 — Legal + collateral
  • Draft MSA + DPA template (UAE-compliant). Hire 1 lawyer for 4 hours; budget AED 5,000.
  • Get tax-deductibility verified by accountant (no marketing claims until confirmed)
  • Refresh the deck (this one) — remove any unverified claims
  • Build pilot dashboard demo with seed data — for sales demos

Kill criteria — abort the program if any of these by end of Week 3

  • Fewer than 10 HR leads agree to a discovery call → demand isn't there yet
  • HR consistently says "we already have Gympass / Wellhub" and won't consider alternatives
  • Lawyer flags major legal issues with handling employee health data under UAE PDPL
  • Dashboard build slips past Week 4 → infrastructure isn't B2B-ready
1
First anchor pilot
Weeks 4-7 · July

Sign one company. Founder-led, hand-delivered. Goal isn't revenue; it's a real reference customer for slide 3 of the deck.

Week 4 — Build the target list
  • 30-company outreach list: friendly CEOs/CHROs in your network first
  • Prioritise warm intros (current ATP members who work at HR-y places)
  • Tier the list: 10 "easy yeses", 10 "stretch", 10 "shots in the dark"
Week 5 — Outreach blitz
  • Personal email or WhatsApp to every name on the list. No mass-blast.
  • Subject line: "Free 30-day wellness pilot for [Company]"
  • One CTA only: 30-min coffee. No deck attached.
  • Daily target: 6 outreach + 2 follow-ups. Track in spreadsheet.
Week 6 — Demos & objection handling
  • Run 5-8 live demos using the dashboard MVP (NOT slides — let them play with it)
  • Collect every objection in a single doc. Look for patterns.
  • If pricing keeps coming up: A/B test fixed-fee (AED 1,500/month flat for 50 employees) vs pay-as-you-move
Week 7 — Sign the first pilot
  • Goal: 1 signed MSA for a 30-day free pilot starting in August
  • Ideal pilot: 50-150 employee company headquartered in Abu Dhabi or Dubai
  • If you can't get to "yes" with anyone by end of Week 7, the messaging is wrong — pause sales and re-interview

Kill criteria — by end of July

  • 0 signed pilots after 15 demos → message-market fit is wrong, rebuild
  • 3+ companies decline citing existing Gympass/Wellhub contracts → competition is bigger problem than expected
  • Pilot signs but immediately wants custom features → not productized; pause to fix
2
Run + learn
Weeks 8-11 · August

Run the first pilot. Operate the unsexy parts: onboarding emails, kickoff session, dashboard tweaks, weekly check-ins. Every failure is a learning, not a panic.

Week 8 — Onboard the first 50
  • Run pilot company's onboarding: CSV upload, welcome emails, magic-link verification
  • Run 1 kickoff company session — founder attends in person
  • Capture activation funnel: % opened email → % clicked → % completed signup → % attended first session
  • Goal: 40% activation by day 14 (industry benchmark for corporate wellness is 12%)
Week 9 — Mid-pilot check-in
  • Day-15 success call with HR contact: what's working, what's broken
  • Survey active employees (5 questions, anonymous)
  • Patch dashboard issues; ship the first wellness report PDF
Week 10 — Quality control on community impact
  • Monitor existing community: are corporate joiners crowding out original members?
  • If any session goes >20% over normal headcount, cap corporate enrollment OR add a duplicate session
  • Survey 20 long-standing members: how has the vibe changed?
Week 11 — Pilot conversion pitch
  • Day-30 results meeting with HR: present the impact report
  • Ask for paid conversion at Growth tier (AED 35/active/month)
  • Get a yes/no/maybe within 7 days
  • Whatever happens, capture a quote for the website: "ATP changed how our team thinks about wellness." — [HR Director, Company]

Kill criteria — by end of August

  • Pilot company activation < 20% by day 15 → product isn't sticky enough
  • Existing community surveys show negative sentiment → corporate program is breaking B2C
  • Pilot company won't convert to paid AND can't articulate why → the value isn't real
3
Launch + repeat
Weeks 12-14 · September · OFFICIAL LAUNCH

Public launch with 1 paying customer as the proof point. Open enrollment for the next 9 founding-pilot slots. Switch from founder-only outreach to a small inbound funnel.

Week 12 — Public launch
  • Publish the corporate deck publicly at /corporate-deck (already live)
  • Launch /corporate sign-up page with self-serve pilot application form
  • LinkedIn announcement from founder, tagged: "ATP Corporative Wellness — born in Abu Dhabi, now open"
  • Press release to local UAE business press (Khaleej Times, The National, Gulf News, Arabian Business)
Week 13 — First case study
  • Publish the anchor pilot case study (with their permission): photos, results, quotes
  • Add their logo to slide 3 of the deck (replacing the "Nike/Audi/Oracle" line)
  • Run a co-branded webinar with the anchor HR contact: "How we got 67% of our team active in 30 days"
Week 14 — Sales engine v0.1
  • Set up a simple CRM (Notion or Airtable, not Salesforce). Track pipeline.
  • Goal: 3 new pilots signed in Sept, 6 by end of Oct
  • Begin discovery conversations with 1-2 health insurers — informational only, no commitments

▸ What NOT to do in the first 90 days

  • ✗ Don't hire a B2B sales rep yet — you need 5 customers before that pays off
  • ✗ Don't build the HRIS API integration — Enterprise tier is a slide, not a real product yet
  • ✗ Don't promise insurance partner discounts — soft "we're in conversation" language only
  • ✗ Don't run a "Founders' Webinar Series" — webinars only matter once you have logos to put on them
  • ✗ Don't spend on paid ads — 0% chance of finding ICP through Meta/Google ads at our scale
  • ✗ Don't open in Saudi / Qatar / other GCC markets — focus is the only edge a 1-person company has
  • ✗ Don't bundle corporate with the existing coach 1-on-1 marketplace — they compete for the same coach time
